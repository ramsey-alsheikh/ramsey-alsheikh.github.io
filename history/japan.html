Japan

Khan Academy style lectures on Japanese-American relations through the Imperial era. After all, who doesn't love some twentieth century history?


     I've always wondered what it would be like to give a lecture, so I tried my hand at it with this video series. Japanese history is fascinating for how quickly their society adapted to radically new political and economical contexts with relatively little instability. What's more, they were one of the very few non-white countries to become a major world power in the twentieth century. The series is split into four parts, each in chronological order. Apologies for the video quality and shaky camera!
